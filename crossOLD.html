They way of the cross

Leader: We adore You, O Christ, and we bless You.
All: Because by Your Holy Cross, You have redeemed the world.

Leaving the house of Caiaphas, where he had been blasphemed, and the house of Herod, where he had been been mocked, Jesus is dragged before Pilate, his back torn with scourges, his head crowned with thorns; and he, who on the last day will judge the living and the dead, is himself condemned to a disgraceful death.
Prayer

It was for us that You did suffer, O Blessed Jesus. It was for our sins that You were condemned to die. Oh, grant that we may detest our sins from the bottom of our hearts, and obtain your mercy and pardon by repentance.

Our Father in Heaven
Hail Mary
Glory Be

Leader: Have mercy on us, O Lord.
All: Have mercy on us.


The Archangel Gabriel announces to Mary that she shall conceive the Son of God.


Mary visits her cousin Elizabeth, who is pregnant with John the Baptist.


Jesus is born.


Mary and Joseph present Jesus in the Temple where they meet Simeon.


After losing Him, Mary and Joseph find young Jesus teaching the Rabbis in the Temple.


Jesus sweats water and blood while praying the night before his passion.


Pilate has Jesus whipped.
Roman soldiers crown Jesus' head with thorns.


Jesus meets His mother and falls three times on the way up Calvary.


Jesus is nailed to the cross and dies before His mother and His apostle John.


Jesus rises from the dead.


Jesus leaves the Apostles and bodily ascends to heaven.


The Apostles receive the Holy Spirit in tongues of fire in the upper room with Mary.


Mary is taken bodily assumed into heaven by God at the end of her life here on earth.


Mary is crowned Queen of Heaven and Earth.


The voice of the Father declares Jesus the beloved Son.


Christ changes water into wine, his first public miracle.


Jesus calls to conversion and forgives the sins of all who draw near to him.


The glory of the Godhead shines forth from the face of Christ.


Jesus offers the first Mass at the Last Supper with his apostles, establishing the sacramental foundation for all Christian living.

Mother of mercy, hail our lifes, our sweetness and our hope. To thee do we cry, poor banished children of Eve: to thee do we send up our sighs, mourning and weeping in this valley of tears. Turn then, most gracious Advocate, thine eyes of mercy toward us, and after this our exile, show unto us the blessed fruit of thy womb, Jesus. O clement, O loving, O sweet Virgin Mary

Pray for us O Holy Mother of God,
That we may be made worthy of the promises of Christ.

 
O God who’s only begotten Son by his life, death, and Resurrection has purchased for us the rewards of eternal life; grant we beseech thee, that meditating on these mysteries of the Most Holy Rosary of the Blessed Virgin Mary, we may imitate what they contain and obtain what they promise through the same Christ our Lord. Amen.
